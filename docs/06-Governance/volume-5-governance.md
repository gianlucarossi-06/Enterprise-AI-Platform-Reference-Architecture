## Executive summary

The governance architecture treats AI capabilities, models, prompts, tools, and policies as controlled enterprise assets.

## Governance principles

Governance is cross-cutting and must remain visible in design, operations, and audit.

## Capability lifecycle

Capabilities move through proposed, approved, active, deprecated, and retired states.

## Model, prompt, and tool governance

Model, prompt, and tool artifacts are versioned and reviewed before production use.

## Policy and approval

Sensitive actions require explicit approval and a recorded decision.

## Compliance and risk

Risk classification influences both routing and approval paths.

## OCI mapping

OCI Identity Domains, Functions, Events, Audit, Logging, Object Storage, and Autonomous Database implement the governance backbone.

## Governance ADRs

The governance ADRs capture the decisions for lifecycle, approvals, and policy management.
